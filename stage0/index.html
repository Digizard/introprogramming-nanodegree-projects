Thinking Like a Programmer

  • Procedural Thinking: For solving a bigger problem, you break down what to do into a series of steps to follow.

  • Abstract Thinking: You take the base understanding and apply it in multiple situations that are similar. For instance, knowing that one apple and one apple are two apples can allow you to understand that one orange and one orange are two oranges. The abstract idea is that 1 + 1 = 2.

  • Systematic Debugging: In an orderly manner, you identify problems (or potential problems). Then, you track down potential causes until you are able to fix the problem.

  • Technological Empathy: Having an understanding for how the computer sees and interprets things.

  • Systems Thinking: You take a big idea, then break it down into its component pieces. Used in planning, allowing you to "divide and conquer".

The Basics of the Web and HTML

World Wide Web

  • Made up of HTML pages.

    • text content: what you see

    • markup: what it looks like

    • references to other documents

    • links to other pages

  • With a computer, a user is connected to the internet and looks up a URL address in the browser. The brower makes an HTTP request. Servers reply to this request with files, which the browser interprets and displays to the user.

HTML Markup

  • Standard Tag: <name>contents<name>

  • With Attribute: <name attr="value">contents<name>

  • Void Tag: <name> or <name attr="value">

Computers are Stupid

Computers follow instructions they are given exactly. Small errors that a human understands the intention of are usually misunderstood by a computer. They might be taught how to guess what to do, but as autocorrect on phones show, it's often not very good to rely on.
